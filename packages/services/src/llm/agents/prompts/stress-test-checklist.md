Walk this checklist when stress-testing a memo. Each item is a question to probe; an item the memo fails to address, supports only with assertion, or contradicts is a candidate concern. Focus on items most exposed in this memo — you don't need to hit every one.

**Founder signal is often thin.** Founder probes depend on signals that are often not online (especially for early-stage founders without prominent public footprints). If `web_search` for a founder returns thin results after one or two queries, stop searching — note the evidence gap and move on. Lean on internal data (meetings, notes, CRM contacts) for Founder probes; reserve web research for Market, Product, GTM, Unit-economics, and Structural-risk probes where public signal is typically stronger.

**Per-section research budget.** Aim for evidence on each section's probes you can support, but cap at 1–2 tool calls per probe area. Prefer `internal_search` first; escalate to `web_search` only when needed. The goal is coverage, not exhaustiveness — a labeled evidence gap is more useful than a 20-call grind.

## Founder
- Tenacity, Evolve-ability, Authenticity, Magnetism — evidence for each, or gap to be addressed.
- Founder/market fit: did they live the problem, or did they pick it off a trends list?
- Technical co-founder present, or proven execution track record in lieu? Note: in the code-is-easy era, design and product judgment matter at least as much as raw engineering depth — probe both.

## Market and problem
- Is the problem acute, or merely "nice to have"?
- Tailwinds: secular vs. cyclical? Distinguish.
- Audience size and willingness to pay — sourced or asserted?
- White space, or replacement of an existing workflow where an incumbent owns distribution?
- Is this a problem felt narrowly today that has the cultural shape to become massive? Or is it already crowded?
- Who are the main competitors, both today and prospectively? Is there a credible basis upon which to compete around the competition?

## Product, design, and defensibility
- Where does Compounding Defensibility come from in 24 months? Identify which of: ecosystem moat, supply/data advantage, switching costs, brand/trust. "We'll build a moat over time" is not an answer.
- Design and UX — does the memo show evidence the team can build a product people enjoy using? Screenshots, NPS, retention curves?
- AI exposure: does this product get better as foundational models improve, or get obsoleted? Could OpenAI/Anthropic/Google make this a feature?
- Multiplayer vs. single-player? Single-player tools rip out easily, multiple stake-holders particularly across companies increase defensibility.
- Is the business doing something AI alone cannot — physical-world execution, regulated trust, supply network, deep workflow integration, marketplace flywheels, network effects? "Difficult to build, easy to explain" is the goal.

## Go-to-market and distribution
- Is the primary acquisition channel paid Meta/Google? That is a tough channel to scale and often becomes prohibitvely expensive.
- Is there at least one Increasing Marginal Returns mechanism — network effects, product-led growth, B2B2C, or flywheel — present and credible at current scale, not just projected?
- Does the product sit in the customer's revenue drivetrain (B2B), or save the customer meaningful time/money (consumer)?
- For consumer: is there a clear monetization mechanism, or audience-first with monetization deferred? 
- For app-layer products in a vibecoded world: what is the distribution moat that prevents a competitor from spinning up a near-clone in a weekend?
- Does the customer have a clear willingness to pay? 

## Unit economics and capital plan
- Runway after the round at current burn — is it 18–24 months?
- Net revenue retention / cohort retention — present, or asserted?
- Do the unit ecomoics make sense?

## Structural and dependency risks
- Third-party infrastructure dependencies (banking partner, credit facility, single cloud, single foundational model, single distribution channel)? Identify and ask what happens if they break 
- Concentration: customer, supplier, geographic, regulatory.
- Platform risk: is the business built on someone else's API, graph, or app store that can be cut off or repriced?

## Valuation and exit
- Comparable exits — what's the realistic exit range, and does the entry price leave room for a fund-returning multiple at our ownership?
- Likely acquirers named and plausible?
- Do you think they can hit required milestones to get the next round raised?
